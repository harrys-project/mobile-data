Scattered snowfall across ET region

(HARRIMAN) Snow began falling Monday night and into Tuesday morning across parts of East Tennessee, prompting some school closures.

Snowfall was reported on the Plateau, and in Knox, Scott, Roane, Campbell and Blount counties.

On the Plateau, up to 2 to 3 inches was expected to accumulate into early Tuesday.

In Morgan County, dispatchers said parts of the roads were snow- and ice-covered, and they were asking drivers to stay home.

In Roane County, sheriff's authorities reported they saw snow and ice Monday night, but that the roads were clear.

While more snow, and perhaps ice, was expected on the Plateau, limited accumulation was expected Monday night in the Knoxville area.

No major road problems were reported in the Knoxville area.

Temperatures were forecast to fall overnight below freezing, creating the chance for slick spots and black ice.

Among the school systems calling off school for Tuesday were those in Campbell, Cumberland, Grainger, Hamblen, Jefferson, Knox, Monroe, Scott and Morgan counties.
