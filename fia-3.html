Road conditions by county Tuesday

Winter weather conditions has prompted a number of school districts to close throughout East Tennessee Tuesday morning.

Road crews spent Monday preparing for the possibility of snow in Knoxville.

Roadways: WBIR Traffic conditions

Up to 2 to 3 inches of snow was expected to accumulate on the plateau into early Tuesday.

More: Latest forecast

Related: Latest school closings

Here's a look at the roadways by county as of 7 a.m. Tuesday:

Anderson County: 30°, light flurries, wet dry, crews on stand by (per TDOT)

Campbell County: Flurries/Snowing. All secondary routes are mostly clear with mostly black pavement showing. Traffic is moving without incidents. Crews continue to treat the roads at this time in areas where patchy spots remain.

Claiborne County: Lots of snow. TDOT crews advise most all routes are clear with some small patches of white but crews report mostly black pavement being seen. Crews

Cocke County:  TDOT crews report the roads are treated and wet.

Cumberland County: Roads pretty slick, city, county and state salt trucks are out, back roads are very slick, interstate is ok, urging drivers to use caution.

Grainger County: Flurries, roads are treated, continuing to treat cold spots, 26°

Greene County: Snow flurries in southeastern part of the county, with 70 S and 107 Houston Valley Rd. still have snow on them and crews are working to clear them.

Hamblen County: Snow has stopped, roads in good condition.

Jefferson County: Snow, roads are treated and slushy, 26°

Monroe County: Flurries with air temp at 27°. Any snow falling is blowing off the roadways.

Morgan County: Patchy snow on roads across the county, crews working to clear the roads. No major traffic incidents, 23°

Scott County:Light flurries continue at this time, but roads are mostly clear. Crews working to treat patchy areas, but traffic moving without incident. 23°

Sevier County: Snow last night, no problems on the roads right now.

Union County: No travel problems at this time.
