Crews fight 1st-alarm fire in northwest Phoenix

PHOENIX - A first-alarm fire broke out at an apartment in northwest Phoenix around 8 a.m. Thursday.

The fire started on the second floor, burning three units before it reached the attic, which covers the entire length of the building, according to the Phoenix Fire Department.

Fire officials said nine units were damaged in total.

One woman had some issues related to smoke inhalation but she refused hospital treatment, according to Phoenix fire officials.

The American Red Cross planned to open a shelter at noon at the Scottsdale School District offices to assist about 80 people affected by the fire.
